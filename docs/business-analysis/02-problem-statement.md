# 02 — Problem Statement

**VERA: Volunteer Emergency Response Alliance**

---

## 2.1 Background

Bangladesh is one of the most densely populated countries in the world. Emergencies occur frequently — road traffic accidents, medical crises, floods, cyclones, fires, and earthquakes. People regularly need blood donations, emergency transport, ambulance services, food, shelter, and rescue support.

Despite this constant demand, **no single national platform** currently connects everyone who needs help with everyone who can provide it. Valuable time is lost at the exact moment when rapid response is critical.

---

## 2.2 Current Situation

Today, emergency assistance in Bangladesh relies heavily on:

| Channel | How it is used | Limitation |
|---------|----------------|------------|
| Facebook groups | Posting urgent requests publicly | Slow, unverified, easy to miss |
| WhatsApp / Messenger | Sharing requests in private groups | Fragmented, no central tracking |
| Phone calls | Calling friends and family | Limited reach, no coordination |
| Word of mouth | Asking neighbours or colleagues | Slow, geographically limited |

Fundraising for medical emergencies and disasters follows the same pattern — scattered posts with little transparency about how funds are used.

During large-scale disasters (floods, cyclones, fires), the problem intensifies. Multiple organizations, volunteers, hospitals, blood banks, and relief teams act independently. Some areas receive duplicate aid while others receive none. Field volunteers cannot easily see which locations have been served and which still need help.

---

## 2.3 Core Problems

### P1 — Emergency Response Is Slow
When people need urgent help, they post on social media or contact friends. This creates delays when every minute matters. There is no structured routing to the nearest available responder.

### P2 — No Central Coordination
Volunteers, hospitals, blood banks, and donors operate independently. Without a shared platform, organizations cannot coordinate deployments, leading to gaps and overlaps in coverage.

### P3 — Difficulty Finding Blood Donors
Patients and families struggle to locate compatible blood donors during medical emergencies. Generic social media posts reach many irrelevant people while missing the right donors.

### P4 — Inefficient Resource Use
Information about who needs help is scattered. The same victim may receive duplicate assistance while another person in greater need receives nothing.

### P5 — Opaque Donations
Donors contributing money or materials during crises rarely know how their contributions are allocated. This reduces trust and willingness to donate again.

### P6 — No Ready Volunteer Network
After disasters strike, organizations waste critical hours assembling rescue teams from scratch. There is no continuously active, verified volunteer pool ready to deploy.

### P7 — Volunteer Opportunity Fragmentation
Students and volunteers seeking opportunities must search across multiple social networks. NGOs lack a single channel to recruit and manage volunteers or issue participation certificates.

---

## 2.4 Key Issues

| Issue | Impact |
|-------|--------|
| People cannot connect quickly | Help arrives too late or not at all |
| Trust in helpers is uncertain | Victims hesitate to accept assistance |
| Donations lack transparency | Reduced donor confidence |
| No location-based tracking | Resources miss underserved areas |
| No request verification | Risk of fake requests and wasted effort |

---

## 2.5 Concerns for the Proposed System

| Concern | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Volunteer verification | Building trust requires identity checks, which take time |
| System overload during disasters | Sudden traffic spikes may delay response |
| Misinformation risk | Centralizing requests without verification could amplify false data |
| Fake requests | Volunteers could be sent to dangerous or non-existent locations |
| Infrastructure failure | Internet and power outages during disasters limit online-only systems |
| Digital literacy gap | Rural, elderly, and low-tech users may be excluded |

---

## 2.6 Opportunities

| Opportunity | How VERA addresses it |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Targeted routing | Blood requests alert only matching nearby donors |
| Ready-to-deploy volunteers | Everyday use keeps the network active before disasters |
| National safety ecosystem | Long-term vision for unified emergency coordination |
| Fair resource distribution | Track coverage and redirect aid to underserved areas |
| Global scalability | Model applicable to other disaster-prone regions |

---

## 2.7 Problem Statement (Formal)

> In Bangladesh, emergency assistance is coordinated through fragmented social media, messaging apps, and personal networks. This causes slow response times, poor resource allocation, difficulty finding blood donors, lack of donation transparency, and uncoordinated disaster relief. There is no centralized, role-based platform that connects citizens, volunteers, NGOs, hospitals, blood banks, and donors — verified and organized — to deliver timely, transparent, and location-aware emergency assistance.

**VERA** is proposed to solve this problem by providing a unified Emergency Assistance and Resource Coordination Information System.

---

## 2.8 Success Criteria

The problem will be considered addressed when VERA demonstrates:

1. Faster connection between help requests and verified responders
2. Measurable reduction in duplicate aid and underserved areas
3. Successful blood donor matching with automated notifications
4. Tracked and transparent donation allocation
5. Active volunteer registration and NGO coordination through one platform
