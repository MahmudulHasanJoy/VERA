# 05 — Interviews

**VERA: Volunteer Emergency Response Alliance**

---

## 5.1 Purpose

Structured interviews were conducted (and planned) with representative stakeholders to understand their experiences with emergency assistance, pain points, and expectations for a centralized platform like VERA.

---

## 5.2 Interview Methodology

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Format** | Semi-structured (open-ended with guided questions) |
| **Duration** | 20–30 minutes per session |
| **Modes** | In-person and online (video call) |
| **Recording** | Notes taken with participant consent |
| **Analysis** | Thematic coding of responses |

---

## 5.3 Interview Guide (Questionnaire)

### Section A — General Experience
1. Have you ever needed emergency help or tried to help someone in an emergency?
2. How did you communicate the need or offer help? (phone, Facebook, WhatsApp, other)
3. How long did it take to get or provide meaningful assistance?
4. What was the most frustrating part of the process?

### Section B — Blood Donation
5. Have you searched for or donated blood during an emergency?
6. How were blood donors found? Was the blood group match confirmed quickly?
7. Would you register on a platform that alerts you only when your blood type is needed nearby?

### Section C — Disaster / NGO Coordination
8. Have you participated in or witnessed disaster relief operations?
9. Were there areas that received too much or too little help? Why?
10. How do NGOs currently recruit and coordinate volunteers?

### Section D — Trust & Technology
11. Would you trust a platform that verifies volunteers with NID/Passport?
12. What concerns do you have about posting emergencies online?
13. What features would make you use VERA regularly?

## 5.5 Interview Findings

### Theme 1 — Slow and Fragmented Communication
> *"We posted on three Facebook groups and called ten people. It took over four hours to find a B+ donor."* — INT-01 (Citizen)

- All participants confirmed social media and phone calls as primary channels
- Average perceived delay for blood requests: 2–6 hours
- No participant could name a single platform used for all emergency types

### Theme 2 — Strong Demand for Blood Matching
> *"I get tagged in every blood post even when the group doesn't match mine. A smart alert system would be much better."* — INT-03 (Blood donor)

- 5/6 participants supported blood-group-targeted notifications
- Donors willing to register if alerts are relevant and not spam

### Theme 3 — Coordination Gaps During Disasters
> *"Two NGOs brought rice to the same ward while the next union got nothing. Nobody had a shared map."* — INT-02 (Volunteer)

- Volunteers and NGO staff described duplicate and missed coverage
- Strong support for coverage monitoring and underserved area alerts

### Theme 4 — Trust Requires Verification
> *"I would only send volunteers to a location if the request is verified. Fake posts are a real risk."* — INT-04 (NGO)

- 6/6 participants raised trust or fake request concerns
- NID/Passport verification seen as necessary for volunteer roles
- Request verification by NGOs/admins strongly supported

### Theme 5 — Donation Transparency
> *"People donate on bKash but never know if it reached the patient. Tracking would encourage more giving."* — INT-01 (Citizen)

- Donors want to see allocation records
- Fundraising campaigns with progress bars and usage reports desired

### Theme 6 — Student Volunteer Demand
> *"I search Facebook every week for volunteer programs. A single listing with certificates would help my career."* — INT-06 (Student)

- Students want structured opportunities and official certificates
- NGOs want a single recruitment channel

### Theme 7 — Accessibility Concerns
> *"My mother cannot use apps. Someone would need to post on her behalf."* — INT-05 (Hospital staff)

- Elderly and low-literacy users need simple UI or proxy posting
- Hospital staff could act as intermediaries for patients

---

## 5.6 Requirements Derived from Interviews

| Interview insight | VERA feature |
|-------------------|--------------|
| Slow blood donor search | Blood request + donor search + notifications |
| Duplicate relief efforts | Disaster coverage monitoring |
| Trust concerns | Volunteer verification + request verification |
| Donation opacity | Donation tracking + fundraising campaigns |
| Volunteer recruitment gap | Opportunity management + certificates |
| Accessibility | Simple UI, phone contact fields, future SMS support |

---

## 5.7 Interview Limitations

- Sample size: 6 participants (pilot phase)
- Geographic concentration: primarily Dhaka and nearby areas
- Self-reported data may not capture all rural perspectives
- Expanded interviews planned in future phases
